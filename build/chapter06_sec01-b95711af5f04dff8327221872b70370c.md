# Derivatives of Elementary Functions

Computing a derivative from the limit definition every time would be far too slow for practical work. Instead, we derive the result once for each family of elementary functions and collect these results in a reference table. The functions we need are exactly those we built up in chapters 3 and 4: power functions, the exponential function, the natural logarithm, the trigonometric functions, and the hyperbolic functions.

```{admonition} Learning goals
:class: attention
* [ ] You know the derivatives of the **power functions**: $(x^n)' = n\,x^{n-1}$ for $n \in \mathbb{R}$.
* [ ] You know the derivatives of the **exponential function** $(e^x)' = e^x$ and the **natural logarithm** $(\ln x)' = \dfrac{1}{x}$.
* [ ] You know the derivatives of the **trigonometric functions** $(\sin x)' = \cos x$, $(\cos x)' = -\sin x$, and $(\tan x)' = \dfrac{1}{\cos^2 x}$.
* [ ] You know the derivatives of the **hyperbolic functions** $(\sinh x)' = \cosh x$, $(\cosh x)' = \sinh x$, and $(\tanh x)' = \dfrac{1}{\cosh^2 x}$.
```
