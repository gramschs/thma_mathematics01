# Product Rule and Quotient Rule

Two functions can also be combined by multiplication or division. The derivative of a product is not simply the product of the individual derivatives, and the same is true for quotients. Both cases require their own rules, which we derive in this section. Together with the sum and factor rules from the previous section, they complete the toolkit for differentiating algebraic combinations of functions.

```{admonition} Learning goals
:class: attention
* [ ] You know the **product rule** $\bigl(f \cdot g\bigr)' = f' \cdot g + f \cdot g'$ and can apply it to products of elementary functions.
* [ ] You know the **quotient rule** $\left(\dfrac{f}{g}\right)' = \dfrac{f' \cdot g - f \cdot g'}{g^2}$ and can apply it wherever $g \neq 0$.
* [ ] You can identify when the product or quotient rule is needed and carry out the differentiation correctly, including simplification of the result.
```
