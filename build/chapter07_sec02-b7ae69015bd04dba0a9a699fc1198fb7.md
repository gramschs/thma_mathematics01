# Velocity, Acceleration, and Jerk

Higher-order derivatives have a direct physical meaning that every engineer encounters. A vehicle's position is a function of time, its velocity is the first derivative of position, and its acceleration is the second. Each derivative adds a layer of information about the motion, and each has practical significance in the design of vehicles, drivetrains, and control systems. The third derivative also has a name and a role in engineering that is worth knowing.

```{admonition} Learning goals
:class: attention
* [ ] You can interpret the first derivative of a position function as **velocity** and the second derivative as **acceleration**.
* [ ] You know the **third derivative** of position as the rate of change of acceleration, called **jerk**, and you understand its significance in ride comfort and structural loading.
* [ ] You can compute velocity, acceleration, and jerk from a given position function and interpret each result physically.
```
