# Local and Global Extrema

Finding the highest or lowest value of a quantity is one of the most common tasks in engineering design. We want the lightest structure that still carries the load, the most efficient operating point for a machine, or the configuration that minimizes energy consumption. Differential calculus provides the systematic tools for locating these optimal values through the concept of extrema, and the necessary groundwork in derivatives and second derivatives is now fully in place.

```{admonition} Learning goals
:class: attention
* [ ] You know the definitions of **local maximum**, **local minimum**, and **global extremum** of a function.
* [ ] You know the **necessary condition** for a local extremum: $f'(x_0) = 0$ at a **critical point** $x_0$.
* [ ] You know the **sufficient conditions** using the second derivative: $f''(x_0) > 0$ implies a local minimum and $f''(x_0) < 0$ implies a local maximum at a critical point.
* [ ] You can find and classify the local extrema of a given differentiable function and determine which of them is the global extremum on a closed interval.
```
