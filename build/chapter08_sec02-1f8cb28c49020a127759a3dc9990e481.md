# Curve Sketching

We now have all the tools needed to draw a qualitatively accurate graph of any differentiable function without plotting individual points. Zeros, monotonicity intervals, extrema, convexity, and inflection points together give a complete picture of the function's behavior. Combining all these tools into one systematic procedure is the technique of curve sketching, and it brings together nearly everything we have developed in this chapter.

```{admonition} Learning goals
:class: attention
* [ ] You can carry out a systematic **curve sketching** procedure: determining the domain, zeros, $y$-intercept, monotonicity intervals, local extrema, convexity intervals, and inflection points.
* [ ] You can produce a qualitatively correct sketch of a differentiable function using only the information obtained from $f$, $f'$, and $f''$.
* [ ] You understand the relationships between the graphs of $f$, $f'$, and $f''$, and you can read information about one from the graph of another.
```
