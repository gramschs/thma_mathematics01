# Net Change and Displacement

```{admonition} Learning goals
:class: attention
* [ ] You know the **Net Change Theorem**: the integral of a rate of change
  over $[a, b]$ equals the net change in the quantity over that interval,
  $\int_a^b F'(x)\,dx = F(b) - F(a)$.
* [ ] You can distinguish between **net displacement** and **total distance
  travelled** for a moving body, and set up the correct integral for each.
* [ ] You can apply the net change theorem to engineering quantities such as
  charge accumulated from a current, or energy delivered by a time-varying
  power signal.
```
