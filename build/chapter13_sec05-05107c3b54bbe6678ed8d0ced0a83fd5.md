# The Complex Conjugate

```{admonition} Learning goals
:class: attention
* [ ] You know the definition of the **complex conjugate** $\bar{z} = a - bi$
  and can represent it geometrically as the reflection of $z$ across the real
  axis.
* [ ] You know the key identities involving the conjugate: $z\bar{z} = |z|^2$,
  $\overline{z + w} = \bar{z} + \bar{w}$, and $\overline{zw} = \bar{z}\bar{w}$.
* [ ] You can use the conjugate to extract the real and imaginary parts of a
  complex number via $\operatorname{Re}(z) = \frac{z + \bar{z}}{2}$ and
  $\operatorname{Im}(z) = \frac{z - \bar{z}}{2i}$, and to perform division in
  Cartesian form.
```
