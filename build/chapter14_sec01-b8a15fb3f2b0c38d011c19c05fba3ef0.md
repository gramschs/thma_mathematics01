# The Polar Form of a Complex Number

```{admonition} Learning goals
:class: attention
* [ ] You know the **polar form** $z = r(\cos\varphi + i\sin\varphi)$, where
  $r = |z|$ is the modulus and $\varphi = \arg(z)$ is the argument introduced
  in Section 13.4.
* [ ] You can write a complex number in polar form given its modulus and
  argument, and interpret the polar form geometrically in the complex plane.
* [ ] You are familiar with the convention that the **principal argument**
  $\varphi$ is chosen in the interval $(-\pi, \pi]$, and you understand why
  the argument is only determined up to multiples of $2\pi$.
```
