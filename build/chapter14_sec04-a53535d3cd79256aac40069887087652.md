```{admonition} Learning goals
:class: attention
* [ ] You know that multiplying two complex numbers in polar form
  $z_1 = r_1 e^{i\varphi_1}$ and $z_2 = r_2 e^{i\varphi_2}$ gives
  $z_1 z_2 = r_1 r_2\, e^{i(\varphi_1 + \varphi_2)}$: moduli multiply and
  arguments add.
* [ ] You know the corresponding rule for division: moduli divide and
  arguments subtract.
* [ ] You can interpret multiplication by $e^{i\varphi}$ geometrically as a
  **rotation** by the angle $\varphi$ in the complex plane, and use this
  interpretation to explain why polar form is natural for problems involving
  rotations and phase shifts in engineering.
```
