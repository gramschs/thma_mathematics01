# Trigonometric Functions as Complex Exponentials

```{admonition} Learning goals
:class: attention
* [ ] You can derive the expressions $\cos\varphi = \dfrac{e^{i\varphi} +
  e^{-i\varphi}}{2}$ and $\sin\varphi = \dfrac{e^{i\varphi} -
  e^{-i\varphi}}{2i}$ directly from Euler's formula and its conjugate.
* [ ] You are familiar with the structural analogy between these formulas and
  the definitions of the hyperbolic functions $\cosh$ and $\sinh$ from
  Section 4.6, with $e^{i\varphi}$ replacing $e^\varphi$.
* [ ] You can use the complex exponential representation to re-derive
  trigonometric identities such as the addition theorems from Section 4.5 by
  algebraic manipulation of exponentials.
```
