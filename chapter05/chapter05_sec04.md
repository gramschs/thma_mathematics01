# Differentiability and Continuity

The limit that defines the derivative does not always exist. A position function with a sudden jump has no well-defined instantaneous velocity at the jump, and a curve with a sharp corner has no unique tangent direction at the corner. Understanding when a function has a derivative, and when it does not, prevents us from applying calculus tools where they cannot be justified.

```{admonition} Learning goals
:class: attention
* [ ] You know the definition of **differentiability** at a point and on an open interval.
* [ ] You know that every differentiable function is also **continuous**, and you understand why the converse is not true in general.
* [ ] You can identify points where a function is continuous but not differentiable, such as **corners**, **cusps**, and points with a vertical tangent.
```
