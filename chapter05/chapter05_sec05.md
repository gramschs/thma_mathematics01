# The Tangent Line

With the derivative defined we can now answer the geometric question that opened this chapter: what is the straight line that just touches the graph of a function at a given point and matches its direction there? The answer uses the derivative directly and gives us our first practical tool of differential calculus. It will reappear in a more powerful form when we study Taylor approximations later in chapter 8.

```{admonition} Learning goals
:class: attention
* [ ] You can write down the equation of the **tangent line** to the graph of $f$ at a point $(x_0, f(x_0))$:
  \begin{equation*}
  t(x) = f(x_0) + f'(x_0)\,(x - x_0).
  \end{equation*}
* [ ] You understand the tangent line as a **linear approximation** of the function in a neighborhood of $x_0$.
* [ ] You can compute the tangent line for a given function and a given point, and interpret its slope and intercept in the context of the application.
```
