```{admonition} Learning goals
:class: attention
* [ ] You know the statement of the **Fundamental Theorem of Calculus**:
  if $F$ is an antiderivative of $f$ on $[a, b]$, then
  $\int_a^b f(x)\,dx = F(b) - F(a)$.
* [ ] You understand why the theorem connects the two branches of calculus,
  differentiation and integration, that were developed independently in
  Chapters 5 through 9.
* [ ] You can use the standard notation $\bigl[F(x)\bigr]_a^b$ for the
  evaluation of an antiderivative at the limits of integration.
```
