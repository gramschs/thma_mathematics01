```{admonition} Learning goals
:class: attention
* [ ] You know **Euler's formula** $e^{i\varphi} = \cos\varphi + i\sin\varphi$
  and can use it to write the polar form of a complex number compactly as
  $z = re^{i\varphi}$.
* [ ] You are familiar with the structural parallel between Euler's formula
  and the hyperbolic identities $\cosh(x) = (e^x + e^{-x})/2$ and
  $\sinh(x) = (e^x - e^{-x})/2$ from Section 4.6.
* [ ] You know **Euler's identity** $e^{i\pi} + 1 = 0$ as the special case
  $\varphi = \pi$ and can derive it directly from Euler's formula.
```
